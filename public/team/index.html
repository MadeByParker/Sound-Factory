George Shelton

CEO (Studio Producer)

Building on a passion for music and a career expanding over more than a decade in education, George has taken on a variety of roles within primary and secondary schools working within mainstream and special schools. From running music therapeutic interventions, to qualifying and teaching as a secondary school music teacher, George has delivered music lessons taking on various styles and group sizes. From winning music awards, mentoring bands on to win competitions, to mixing and mastering their music, George has experience in many fields.

He has taken on artist development work outside of education working at the Barbican Theatre, with musicians performing at The Milleniums Awakening nights, and has spent years playing in touring indie rock/pop bands. Songs he has written have seen both TV and Radio play, as well as BBC sessions and festival stages across the UK. He has a wide range of knowledge from working with some of the UK’s best producers and engineers and has recorded in some of the most historic and prestigious studios around.

Whilst working within education, George noticed a lack in other music school’s focus on skill acquisition and song writing. He also knew the importance and excitement that studio time offered and so this was incorporated into Sound Factory Music School’s process. His passion for creating young artists and giving young people the skills to express themselves through music is something that kick started Sound Factory into providing a music school. As someone who grew up as a ‘Pupil Premium’ student, George is also passionate about giving back to those who can’t afford music tuition and therefore created a scheme that involves all students across all backgrounds.

John Corfield

Associate Director of Sound Production & Engineering

John Cornfield has produced and engineered some of the biggest names in music - Muse, Oasis, Robert Plant, Supergrass, Stone Roses, The Verve and many more. You name them, he’s worked with them, mostly at the legendary Sawmills Studio in Cornwall.

In his early years, production legend John Cornfield started out doing sound and lighting at school and went on to an engineering apprenticeship where he picked up some valuable mechanical and electronic skills. He then spent time fixing things for bands, including equipment, lighting rigs and even vans whilst on the road. After working closely with one of the many Hawkwind drummers, Martin Griffin, John was put in touch with Dennis Smith and Simon Fraser at the prestigious Sawmills Studio where he became an assistant engineer working with the likes of John Leckie (Engineer/Tape operator for artists such as: Pink Floyd, John Lennon, Paul McCartney, George Harrsion) before setting out on the freelance path leading him to produce now-classic albums for the likes of Oasis, Robert Plant, Muse, New Model Army, Supergrass, Verve, The Stone Roses and many more. John still prefers the energy of musicians playing together: “I am into recording bands as bands and prefer capturing the live energy that a band produces when they are on it”.

From decades working in the music industry and across dozens of studios up and down the country, John brings with him an abundance of experience and knowledge that few could match. John is an integral part of the Sound Factory team, advising us on the best recording equipment available and informing us of best practice when mixing and mastering the music we record. Our studio will be heavily influenced by John’s guidance over time, so that we can give clients and students the best experience and mixes possible.

Luke Riley

Music Practitioner & Artist Development Coordinator

Luke graduated with a degree in Songwriting from the University of Sussex propelling him into Brightons music scene. Here he took on Artist Development work whilst continuing to stay active as a touring musician. He has written music that has seen festival stages, radio, and television, as well as recording in some of the most prestigious and historic recording studios the UK has to offer. Luke specializes in lyric writing and artist development, and has years of experience playing guitar and singing.

During his time in the music industry, Luke has run music nights across many different venues, working with different artists and engineers. His time in studios have seen Luke work alongside some of the best producers in the industry, and this has shed light on the mixing process that Luke is now very familiar with. Luke continues to stay active as a performer, and is in the process of writing and recording his debut album after years of working as a frontman in former bands; all of which honing his knowledge of the industry.

Luke has also worked as a music technician in a mainstream secondary school, giving him an insight into music education and the various needs and demands schools face. Since then, Luke has developed a passion for education, and is currently in the process of applying for a PGCE. Since joining the Sound Factory team at our launch, Luke’s drive and desire to develop all three sectors have influenced growth and productivity in every area. All attributes and skills listed, alongside the creative mind Luke poses, highlights our team’s ability here at Sound Factory.

Luke Riley

Sound Factory Live Engineer

Joss started his Career in the Music industry after completing his Degree in Audio & Music Production. From this, Joss created opportunities to engineer front of house at live music venues and worked with various bands within different genres. Joss continued to progress and engineer for bands within recording studios, working relentlessly to facilitate optimum efficiency (time is money for artists!) and quality mixes for clients.

Joss has been playing guitar and bass since his early teenage years, and has taken on various roles within touring bands. He has played on tracks that have seen festival stages, radio and television, all of which highlights his talent and skill as a musician. Due to this experience, Joss has worked alongside some of the best producers/engineers in the South West, thus bringing with him a wealth of knowledge, and tips and tricks of the trade. He also has ten years worth of experience managing staff and working within the customer service industry.

More recently Joss has started work at the University of Plymouth as an AV Technician, and is now part of the Sound Factory Team too. Joss gives us expert advice and works with us as a live sound and studio engineer. As an advocate for a change in music education, Joss sees and supports the impact Sound Factory Music School has on students, and continues to work with us to provide the best possible live and studio engineering experience available.

Harry Parker

Head of Website Design and Marketing Executive

Graduating with a degree in Computer Science, specialising in Cyber Security, Harry has always been driven by his desire to make a meaningful impact on people's lives through the digital realm. As technology becomes increasingly pervasive, he recognised the immense potential it holds for improving the way we connect and engage with the world.

Throughout his academic journey, Harry's passion for creating visually stunning and highly functional websites has grown. He honed his exceptional talents and meticulous attention to detail, enabling him to design captivating online experiences for a wide array of small businesses and individuals. Notable among his portfolio are successful projects like The South Hams Society and Eyes of Caelum, where Harry's innovative approach and commitment to excellence shone through.

From an early age, Harry's fascination with computers has created a deep-seated ambition to harness his IT skills for the betterment of society. His dedication to improving people's lives through technology is evident in his four years of experience in web design, both within and beyond the university setting. This rich background has provided him with a wealth of knowledge and expertise in crafting tailor-made websites that precisely meet the unique needs and aspirations of his clients.
